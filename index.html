Chris

Chris Johnstone has been a school teacher for over 30 years and has taught yoga since 2015. Chris completed his yoga teacher training in Rishikesh India and additional yoga courses including yin yoga. Chris believes that yoga is for everyone irrespective of gender, age, flexibility, balance and strength as practitioners work within their own body's limitations. Chris is passionate about the mental and physical benefits of regular yoga practise.

Uschi

Uschi is constantly in awe of the miracle that is our body-mind-heart. She loves nothing more than meeting others in movement, connection and exploration of this beautiful lived experience. Her journey into movement medicine began in 2008 with a full certification in clinical pilates This opened her to the world of mind-body connection and the wisdom of the body. After running her own small pilates studio for a few years she set sail overseas and followed her curiosity and joy into an ongoing study of yoga, conscious dance, thai yoga massage, craniosacral therapy, KaHuna massage and ZenThai Shiatsu. Uschi offers massage in Mansfield via her business Wild Essence Healing; and runs local women's circles and nature-based experiences. When not working she can often be found exploring mountains and streams; hands in the veg patch; or with paint-brush flowing at home.

Class description: Uschi's classes are a slow flowing blend of alignment based vinyasa, pilates principles, mindfulness and breath. Grounding and centring, the sessions are a place to connect, breathe, move and come home to your body, your environment and your heart. Suitable for both beginners and experienced practitioners. www.windessencehealing.com

Leith

Leith is originally from Aotearoa New Zealand and moved back to Mansfield at the start of 2021. Leith first starting regularly practicing yoga in 2006 and completed her first 200hr Vinyasa training in 2012 while living here in Mansfield through SomaChi, a Melbourne studio. This teaching's beautiful, grounded, and dynamic sequencing, as well as the accompanying Vippasana based meditation are foundations that have stayed with her.

In between then and now Leith has practiced yoga in the different places she has lived (Germany, Rwanda and Thailand) and many others travelled to along the way. This included practicing throughout the entirety of both pregnancies with her two young children and postnatally. Yoga's physicality, breathwork, spiritual teachings, long lineages of wisdom, invitations to sit with challenge and generous community have stood her in strong stead through many big changes and life's day-to-day.

In 2020, before returning to Mansfield, Leith completed a second 200hr Vinyasa teacher training through Awhi Yoga and Wellbeing in Te Whanganui-a-tara/Wellington, Aotearoa NZ. Two significant focuses of this course were the furthering of a deep sense of service as Yoga teachers and the exploration of affinities between the ancient teachings from which Yoga originated and those of Te Ao Māori (the Māori worldview). She looks forward to sharing all she has learnt, loves and is forever discovering more about in class.
